Expungement & License Reinstatement

Mistakes shouldn’t define your future. The Dollard Firm helps clients across Indiana clear their criminal records and restore their driver’s licenses — providing the guidance you need to move forward with confidence.

Indiana Expungement Law

Indiana’s “Second Chance Law” allows many people to seal or expunge old criminal convictions, arrests, or dismissed charges. This improves background checks, employment applications, and housing opportunities.

Our attorneys assess eligibility, prepare filings, and represent clients throughout the hearing process.

  • Expungement for misdemeanors and certain felonies
  • Record sealing for dismissed or non-conviction cases
  • Restoration of firearm rights (where applicable)
  • Protection from background check discrimination
  • Improved access to employment and housing

License Reinstatement Assistance

A suspended or revoked license can disrupt your life and livelihood. We assist with BMV reinstatement, hardship licenses, and specialized driving privileges.

  • Hardship and probationary driver’s licenses
  • Reinstatement after DUI, OWI, or unpaid fines
  • Representation in BMV administrative hearings
  • Driving privilege appeals and error correction
  • Reinstatement petitions for CDL and commercial drivers

Whether your license was suspended due to an OWI, unpaid tickets, or other offenses, we guide you through the steps to regain your driving rights.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who qualifies for expungement in Indiana?

Eligibility depends on conviction type, sentence completion, and time elapsed. Most misdemeanors and lower-level felonies can be expunged after 5–8 years.

What does expungement actually do?

Expungement seals your criminal record from public view, preventing most employers, landlords, and background checks from accessing it.

Can I get my license back after a DUI or OWI?

Yes — many drivers qualify for reinstatement or specialized driving privileges after completing suspensions or treatment programs. We coordinate with the Indiana BMV on your behalf.

How long does the expungement process take?

On average, expungement takes 3–6 months from filing to approval, depending on county and case type. We ensure filings are accurate to avoid unnecessary delays.
