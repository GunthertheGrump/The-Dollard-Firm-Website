Power of Attorney

A Power of Attorney (POA) allows you to appoint someone you trust to make important financial, legal, or healthcare decisions on your behalf. The Dollard Firm helps clients establish clear, enforceable POA documents that protect their autonomy and peace of mind.

Understanding Power of Attorney

A Power of Attorney grants legal authority to another person — called your “agent” or “attorney-in-fact” — to act on your behalf in specific or general matters. This can include managing bank accounts, signing documents, or making medical decisions if you become unable to do so.

At The Dollard Firm, we ensure your POA is customized to your needs, clearly defining your agent’s powers and protecting your wishes under Indiana law.

Types of Power of Attorney

  • General Power of Attorney: Grants broad decision-making authority over your affairs.
  • Durable Power of Attorney: Remains effective even if you become incapacitated.
  • Limited (or Special) Power of Attorney: Restricts authority to specific tasks or time periods.
  • Healthcare Power of Attorney: Appoints someone to make medical decisions if you are unable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why should I have a Power of Attorney?

A Power of Attorney ensures that your financial and personal matters can be handled without court intervention if you’re ever incapacitated or unavailable. It provides stability and clarity for you and your loved ones.

Can I revoke or change my Power of Attorney?

Yes. As long as you are of sound mind, you can revoke or modify your Power of Attorney at any time by creating a new document or providing written notice to your agent and relevant institutions.

What happens if I don’t have a Power of Attorney?

Without a valid POA, your loved ones may need to go through court proceedings to obtain guardianship or conservatorship in order to manage your affairs — a process that can be time-consuming and costly.

Who should I choose as my agent?

Your agent should be someone you trust completely — reliable, financially responsible, and willing to act in your best interests with honesty and transparency.
