Frequently Asked Questions

What is considered a felony in Indiana?

Felonies in Indiana are crimes punishable by more than one year in state prison, ranging from Level 6 to Level 1 based on severity.

Can felony charges be reduced or dismissed?

Through negotiations, evidentiary challenges, or procedural motions, skilled defense attorneys can often reduce felony charges or secure dismissal.

What are possible penalties for felony convictions?

Penalties can include imprisonment, probation, fines, restitution, and loss of civil rights.

Can a felony conviction be expunged in Indiana?

Some felony convictions can be expunged after a waiting period if conditions are met and no new offenses occur.
