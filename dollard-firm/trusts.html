Trusts

Trusts are powerful legal tools that allow you to manage, protect, and distribute your assets with precision and privacy. The Dollard Firm helps individuals and families across Indiana establish tailored trust structures that ensure financial security and honor your long-term intentions.

Understanding Trusts and Their Benefits

A trust allows you to transfer assets to a trustee who manages them for the benefit of your chosen beneficiaries. Unlike a will, trusts can help your loved ones avoid probate, maintain privacy, and provide ongoing management of your assets during your lifetime and beyond.

The Dollard Firm works closely with you to design a trust that reflects your financial goals, protects your family, and minimizes future complications or disputes.

Frequently Asked Questions

What’s the difference between a will and a trust?

A will outlines how your assets should be distributed after death, while a trust allows for ongoing management of your assets during your lifetime and beyond—often without the need for probate.

Do I need a trust if I already have a will?

A will is essential, but a trust offers greater flexibility and control, especially for larger estates, blended families, or those seeking to avoid probate and protect privacy.

How can a trust protect my family’s assets?

Trusts can shield assets from creditors, minimize estate taxes, and ensure that your beneficiaries receive support according to your specific terms, rather than all at once or through court proceedings.

Who should serve as my trustee?

A trustee should be someone you trust implicitly—an individual or institution capable of responsibly managing assets and following your instructions with care, honesty, and impartiality.
