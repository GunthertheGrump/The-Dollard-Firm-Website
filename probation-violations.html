Understanding Probation Violations

Probation violations can include missing check-ins, failing drug tests, committing new crimes, or not following court-ordered conditions. Consequences can be serious, including jail time or extended probation.

With Mr. Dollard’s firsthand knowledge of how probation officers evaluate compliance, The Dollard Firm builds strong, strategic defenses that focus on mitigating penalties and preventing incarceration.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is considered a probation violation?

A probation violation occurs when you fail to comply with any conditions of your probation set by the court. This could include missed meetings, new arrests, or failing required programs or tests.

What are the consequences?

Penalties can include extended probation, fines, mandatory programs, or incarceration. An experienced attorney can often negotiate lesser consequences by showing compliance progress or mitigating factors.

Can I avoid jail?

Yes. The Dollard Firm routinely advocates for alternatives such as community service, treatment programs, or modified supervision instead of incarceration.
