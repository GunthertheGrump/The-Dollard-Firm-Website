Frequently Asked Questions

What is estate planning and why is it important?

Estate planning is the process of legally organizing how your assets will be managed and distributed after your passing. It ensures your loved ones are cared for and prevents disputes or confusion during probate.

Do I need a will or a trust?

Both tools serve different purposes — a will directs how your property is distributed, while a trust can help avoid probate and provide more control over your assets. We can help determine which option fits your needs best.

How long does probate take in Indiana?

Probate typically takes between six months to a year, depending on the size of the estate and whether disputes arise. Our firm works to streamline this process and resolve issues efficiently.

Can an attorney help prevent family disputes?

Yes. A well-drafted estate plan reduces ambiguity and helps families avoid conflict. Our attorneys create clear, enforceable documents that protect your intentions and minimize legal challenges.
