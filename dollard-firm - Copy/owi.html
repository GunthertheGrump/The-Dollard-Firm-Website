Comprehensive DUI / OWI Defense in Indiana

Indiana law defines Operating While Intoxicated (OWI) as operating a vehicle with a blood alcohol concentration (BAC) of 0.08% or higher. Even a first-time DUI charge can result in fines, license suspension, and criminal records that impact your career and insurance rates.

The Dollard Firm examines every detail—your traffic stop, field sobriety testing, and chemical test procedures—to identify violations of your constitutional rights and procedural errors by law enforcement.

  • Inaccurate BAC testing and machine calibration issues

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens after a DUI arrest in Indiana?

After an OWI arrest, your license may be suspended immediately. You’ll face both criminal charges and administrative penalties. The sooner you contact The Dollard Firm, the faster we can preserve your rights and challenge evidence.

Can I refuse a breath or blood test?

Refusing a chemical test often leads to automatic license suspension under Indiana’s implied consent law. However, these suspensions can sometimes be appealed if proper police procedures weren’t followed.

How long will a DUI stay on my record?

A DUI or OWI conviction in Indiana remains on your record indefinitely. However, expungement may be possible after a waiting period. The Dollard Firm also assists with criminal record expungement and driver’s license reinstatement.

Do I need an attorney for a first-time DUI?

Absolutely. Even a first-time offense can carry long-term penalties. Our attorneys negotiate with prosecutors, challenge test results, and help prevent permanent damage to your driving and criminal record.
