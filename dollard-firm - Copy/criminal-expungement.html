Indiana Expungement Law

Indiana’s “Second Chance Law” allows many people to seal or expunge old criminal convictions, arrests, or dismissed charges from their public record. This can make background checks, employment applications, and housing opportunities far easier.

Our attorneys carefully assess your eligibility, prepare all necessary filings, and represent you throughout the expungement hearing process to ensure your record is cleared properly.

  • Expungement for misdemeanors and certain felonies
  • Record sealing for dismissed or non-conviction cases
  • Restoration of firearm rights (where applicable)
  • Protection from background check discrimination
  • Improved access to employment and housing

License Reinstatement Assistance

A suspended or revoked license can disrupt your life and livelihood. The Dollard Firm assists with Indiana Bureau of Motor Vehicles (BMV) reinstatement, hardship licenses, and specialized driving privileges.

  • Hardship and probationary driver’s licenses
  • Reinstatement after DUI, OWI, or unpaid fines
  • Representation in BMV administrative hearings
  • Driving privilege appeals and error correction
  • Reinstatement petitions for CDL and commercial drivers

Whether your license was suspended due to an OWI, unpaid tickets, or other offenses, we’ll guide you through the steps to legally and efficiently regain your driving rights.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who qualifies for expungement in Indiana?

Eligibility depends on your conviction type, sentence completion, and how much time has passed. Most misdemeanors and lower-level felonies can be expunged after 5–8 years. We’ll review your record to determine exact eligibility.

What does expungement actually do?

Expungement seals your criminal record from public view, meaning employers, landlords, and most background checks cannot access it. Law enforcement and courts retain limited access.

Can I get my license back after a DUI or OWI?

Yes — many drivers qualify for reinstatement or specialized driving privileges after completing suspensions or treatment programs. Our attorneys handle petitions and coordinate with the Indiana BMV for you.

How long does the expungement process take?

On average, expungement takes between 3–6 months from filing to approval, depending on the county and case type. Our firm ensures filings are accurate and complete to avoid unnecessary delays.
