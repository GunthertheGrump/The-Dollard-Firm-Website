False Arrests

Everyone has the right to freedom from unlawful detention. The Dollard Firm represents clients in Indiana who have been falsely arrested, helping them seek justice and protect their rights.

Understanding False Arrest

A false arrest occurs when an individual is detained without proper legal authority or probable cause. Our attorneys evaluate the circumstances of your arrest to determine if your rights were violated.

  • Analysis of probable cause and legal procedures
  • Investigation of police misconduct or errors
  • Representation in civil claims for damages
  • Protection of your civil rights
  • Advice on minimizing long-term consequences

Legal Remedies & Representation

If you were falsely arrested, you may be entitled to damages or other remedies. The Dollard Firm advocates aggressively on behalf of clients, seeking justice through negotiation, settlement, or litigation.

  • Filing civil rights claims against responsible parties
  • Negotiation and settlement of wrongful arrest cases
  • Representation in court and administrative proceedings
  • Protection against retaliation or future harm
  • Guidance on clearing records or expungement if needed

We work to ensure that clients receive fair compensation and accountability while restoring their peace of mind and reputation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifies as a false arrest?

A false arrest occurs when law enforcement detains someone without probable cause, a warrant, or legal justification.

Can I sue for damages?

Yes. Victims of false arrest may be entitled to compensation for emotional distress, lost wages, legal costs, and other damages.

Do I need a lawyer?

Legal representation is strongly recommended. An experienced attorney can evaluate your case, navigate complex procedures, and protect your rights.

How long do false arrest cases take?

Case timelines vary based on complexity, investigation, and court schedules. We work efficiently to pursue justice while protecting your interests.
