Sentence Modifications in Indiana

The Dollard Firm assists clients seeking to reduce jail time, adjust probation terms, or gain access to community corrections programs. Serving Noblesville and surrounding Indiana counties, we guide you through every step of post-conviction modification.

What is a Sentence Modification?

A sentence modification allows a court to reconsider a punishment after conviction. Indiana law gives individuals the opportunity to request adjustments to their sentences, such as early release, reduced supervision, or a shift to community corrections.

At The Dollard Firm, we help clients present strong, well-prepared modification petitions to show genuine progress and rehabilitation.

Who is Eligible?

Under Indiana Code 35-38-1-17, eligibility can depend on several factors:

  • The type and severity of the original conviction
  • Time already served and behavior during incarceration
  • Prosecutorial approval or objection
  • Previous modification requests and court discretion

Why Seek a Modification?

Life circumstances evolve — and so should your sentence. Whether you’ve shown consistent rehabilitation, maintained compliance, or faced changed family or employment conditions, seeking a modification could provide relief and a chance to move forward.




Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a sentence modification take?

The process can take a few weeks to several months depending on county, judge, and prosecutor involvement.

Can I request more than once?

In most cases, yes — though limitations exist. Repeat filings may require new grounds or prosecutor agreement.

Do I need a lawyer?

While self-filing is possible, having an experienced attorney improves success. We ensure your petition is prepared and presented effectively.

Can I modify probation instead of jail time?

Yes. Many modifications focus on probation or community corrections, such as reducing reporting or converting to unsupervised status.
