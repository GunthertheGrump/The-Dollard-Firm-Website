Frequently Asked Questions

What is considered a federal crime?

Federal crimes are offenses that violate federal law, including white-collar fraud, drug trafficking, firearms violations, immigration offenses, and crimes that cross state lines. They are prosecuted in federal courts and carry stricter penalties.

How is federal court different from state court?

Federal courts handle violations of federal law. Procedures, sentencing guidelines, and federal prosecutors differ from state courts. Defense strategies require specialized knowledge of federal rules and processes.

Should I hire an attorney immediately after a federal investigation begins?

Yes. Federal investigations can be aggressive and complex. Early legal representation ensures your rights are protected and improves outcomes during negotiations or trial preparation.

Can federal charges be reduced or dismissed?

Yes, through plea negotiations, diversion programs, and challenges to evidence or procedure. The Dollard Firm works diligently to minimize charges and penalties whenever possible.

Facing Federal Charges?

Federal offenses require experienced legal representation. The Dollard Firm is available 24/7 to provide skilled and aggressive defense in federal court.
